# Quality Rubric

Score changed or generated work out of 100. A score cannot override a critical failure.

## Critical failures

Fail delivery until fixed if any condition is true:

- supplied product, person, price, date, rule, Logo, or qualifier was invented, replaced, or altered without authorization;
- content from one reference contaminated another artifact;
- critical Chinese text is gibberish, clipped, obscured, or outside the canvas;
- conditional, rebate, coupon, membership, and instant-discount prices are visually conflated;
- the main subject is unrecognizable or an essential feature is accidentally cropped;
- an edit overwrote the source without explicit permission.

## Weighted score

| Category | Weight | Pass evidence |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Factual fidelity | 20 | Exact copy and required assets match the input; uncertainty is declared |
| Information hierarchy | 20 | Three-second path clearly reveals title, key fact, conversion anchor, and CTA |
| Mobile readability | 15 | Critical and support copy remains readable at 375-390 px and 25% thumbnail preview |
| Composition | 15 | Grid, spacing, crop, scale, and negative space feel intentional; one dominant event |
| Aesthetic specificity | 15 | Visual language fits audience, category, channel, and reference; no generic AI default |
| Image treatment | 10 | Subject role, lighting, white balance, crop, and shadows are coherent |
| Channel and compliance fit | 5 | Ratio, safe zones, promotional wording, and disclaimers fit the target channel |

Recommended delivery threshold: `85/100`, with no critical failure and no category below 60% of its weight.

## Visual proof tests

1. **Thumbnail test**: at 25% size, identify the title and primary event/benefit without zooming.
2. **Three-second test**: read title, date/key fact, conversion anchor, and CTA in the intended order.
3. **Quiet-field test**: every critical text block has controlled local contrast without a competing line, face, product edge, texture, or giant glyph crossing it.
4. **Silhouette test**: blur or squint at the poster; the major massing still has one focal hierarchy.
5. **No-decoration test**: remove any element whose absence does not weaken hierarchy, grouping, navigation, atmosphere, or conversion.
6. **Reference-role test**: verify that each supplied reference contributed only its assigned content/style/character/product/layout role.

## Delivery report

Return:

- mode and selected template;
- output ratio and intended channel;
- aesthetic profile and four dial values;
- preserved invariants;
- material changes;
- rubric score and failed/fixed checks;
- unresolved uncertainty.
